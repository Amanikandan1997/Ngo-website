Our History

Bringing light to the visually challenged since 1970s

In the 1970's, if one visited Rahator Memorial Methodist Tamil Church, Parel, Mumbai, visually challenged people occupying the front pews and worshipping was a common sight. Mrs. Sulochana Retnamony and Rev Dr. S. Retnamony had a vision for them. This vision was put into action with the help of Mr. John Doss, a mission worker, appointed specifically to cater for the needs of the visually challenged people attending church. Even after his demise, his impact continued with the growth in church attendees from the visually challenged community.

In 2006, C. Raj Joseph joined the team. During the difficult Corona period (2020-2022), we as a team were able to serve the visually challenged without any disruption. Soon, we grew to a strong team of 6, working towards rehabilitation and restoration of the visually challenged community. Our focus is on improving the quality of life. We strive to bring them out of their current situation where they and their children felt the need to resort to begging for survival. We have child education as one of our key goals to enable this.

Today, we have grown to a team of 31 dedicated full-time, part-time, and promotional staff who strive hard to share the good news of the gospel of Lord Jesus Christ with the visually Challenged people. This is ably supported by a myriads/host of volunteers and partners from various Churches and NGO's that has enabled us to grow over these last 2 decades. Our office is at Vangani, Thane District. Our presence is in 18 districts of Maharashtra and 9 districts of Tamil Nadu.

Self Sustaining Projects

VVCF identified willing persons from poor socio-economic backgrounds to be trained to make/sell provisions such as floor cleaners, door mats made from donated sarees and paper bowls.

Shelter

Home for the Destitute

VVCF started this Home in February 2020 at Vangani, Thane District with the intention to provide housing with basic amenities for the elderly and visually challenged in the area.

Education

Education Aid

VVCF provides education opportunities for children of visually challenged persons by raising sponsors for their schooling and higher education, and providing guidance for their professional development.
